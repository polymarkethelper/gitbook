# Polls Intel

The **Polls Intel** panel aggregates and displays weighted polling averages from 16+ major pollsters — giving you a data-driven view of public opinion for election and political markets.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/polls-intel.png" alt="Polls Intel Panel"><figcaption>Weighted polling average for a US election market</figcaption></figure>

---

## Overview

Prediction market odds for political events are heavily influenced by polling data. The Polls Intel panel brings polling averages directly into Polymarket so you can instantly compare:

- Where the polls stand
- Where Polymarket odds stand
- Whether there's a mispricing between polling consensus and market prices

---

## Data Sources

Polls are aggregated from **16+ major polling organizations**, including national and state-level pollsters. The panel uses **weighted averages** that account for:

- **Pollster quality rating** — higher-rated pollsters have more influence
- **Sample size** — larger samples get more weight
- **Recency** — newer polls get more weight than older ones
- **Methodology** — likely voter models vs. registered voter models
- **Partisan lean** — historical accuracy adjusted for known biases

This produces a more reliable consensus estimate than any single poll alone.

## What the Panel Shows

### Head-to-Head Averages
For two-candidate races:
- Candidate A polling average (%)
- Candidate B polling average (%)
- Polling margin and trend direction

### Trend Charts
How polling averages have moved over the last:
- 30 days
- 90 days
- Since race began

Trend direction matters as much as the absolute number — a candidate moving +3 points in polls over two weeks is significant even if still behind.

### Individual Poll Breakdown
Expand to see the most recent polls from each organization:
- Pollster name and rating
- Date conducted
- Sample size
- Results

### State-Level Data
For US election markets with state-specific outcomes, the panel shows state-by-state polling averages — critical for electoral college prediction markets.

## Polls vs. Market Odds

One of the most powerful ways to use Polls Intel is comparing the polling average to current Polymarket odds:

| Polling Average | Polymarket Odds | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Candidate A at 52% | Candidate A at 65¢ | Market is pricing A much higher than polls suggest — possible overreaction to narrative |
| Candidate A at 52% | Candidate A at 50¢ | Market roughly matches polls — fairly priced |
| Candidate A at 52% | Candidate A at 40¢ | Market is pricing A much lower than polls — possible buying opportunity if you trust the polls |

{% hint style="warning" %}
Polls are not perfectly predictive. Systematic polling errors (as seen in 2016 and 2020 US elections) can cause large divergences between polling averages and actual outcomes. Use polls as one input among many.
{% endhint %}
