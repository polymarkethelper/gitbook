# Pentagon Activity Tracker (PizzINT)

The **Pentagon Activity Tracker** — internally called **PizzINT** — monitors real-time geopolitical and military activity, providing intelligence signals for markets related to conflicts, diplomatic events, and national security developments.

---

## What It Monitors

The Pentagon Activity Tracker aggregates signals from multiple open-source intelligence (OSINT) channels:

- **Official government statements** — Pentagon, State Department, White House, NATO briefings
- **Military movement reports** — troop deployments, equipment transfers, naval positioning
- **Diplomatic cables** — treaty negotiations, sanctions, bilateral talks
- **Intelligence community leaks** — verified OSINT from established analysts
- **Conflict escalation indicators** — alert levels, troop mobilizations, airspace closures

---

## Key Data Points

### Activity Feed
A real-time timeline of geopolitical events, prioritized by relevance to active Polymarket markets. Each entry includes:
- Event description
- Source and credibility rating
- Timestamp
- Region / country affected
- Link to original source

### Escalation Indicators
Visual indicators showing whether a situation is:
- 🟢 **De-escalating** — diplomatic progress, ceasefire activity
- 🟡 **Stable** — no significant change
- 🔴 **Escalating** — increased military activity, heightened rhetoric

### Region Focus
Filter the feed by region to focus on specific geographic areas relevant to the market you're trading.

## Why "PizzINT"?

The name is a nod to the OSINT community's playful naming conventions — "PizzINT" refers to the practice of reading military signals from open-source data, similar to how analysts once identified nuclear facility activity from pizza delivery patterns near classified sites.

The PolyHelper team uses this as a shorthand for the capability: **everyday open-source data revealing strategic intelligence**.

---

## Use Cases

**Conflict outcome markets** (e.g., "Will [Country] invade [Country] by [date]?")
- Monitor escalation indicators for early signals
- Track troop movements and mobilization reports
- Watch for diplomatic breakthroughs or breakdowns

**Sanctions and policy markets** (e.g., "Will new sanctions be imposed on [Country]?")
- Track diplomatic statements from key actors
- Monitor for precursor events (military incidents, political provocations)

**Leadership markets** (e.g., "Will [Leader] still be in power by [date]?")
- Monitor for coup indicators, internal dissent signals, or health-related reporting

---

## Data Sources

PizzINT pulls from a curated list of high-reliability OSINT analysts and official sources, including:
- ISW (Institute for the Study of War)
- ACLED (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data)
- Official DoD press releases
- Verified geopolitical analysts on X with strong track records
