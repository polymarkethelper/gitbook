# Stocks Context

The **Stocks Context** panel provides market data and analyst intelligence for Polymarket events tied to publicly traded companies — earnings outcomes, stock price targets, M&A activity, and more.

---

## What It Shows

### Live Stock Data
- Current stock price and daily change (%)
- Pre/post market price (when applicable)
- 52-week high and low
- Market capitalization
- P/E ratio and forward P/E

### Analyst Consensus
A summary of Wall Street analyst ratings:

| Rating | Description |
|---|---|
| Strong Buy | Majority of analysts recommend buying |
| Buy | More buys than holds/sells |
| Hold | Mixed analyst opinion |
| Sell / Underperform | Analysts expect the stock to decline |

Includes:
- Number of analysts covering the stock
- Average price target
- Highest and lowest price targets
- Recent upgrades or downgrades

### Earnings Data
For earnings-related markets:
- **Next earnings date** — when the company reports
- **Consensus EPS estimate** — what analysts expect
- **Previous earnings results** — did the company beat or miss in recent quarters?
- **Revenue estimates** vs. guidance
- **Earnings surprise history** — track record of beats vs. misses

Companies that consistently beat earnings estimates tend to pop on report day; chronic misses often drop.

### Insider Activity
- Recent insider buys and sells (SEC Form 4 filings)
- Insider buy/sell ratio over the last 90 days

Heavy insider buying is generally a bullish signal; heavy selling (beyond planned divestments) can be bearish.

## How to Use It

**For earnings outcome markets** (e.g., "Will [Company] beat EPS estimates this quarter?"):
1. Check the earnings surprise history — does this company typically beat estimates?
2. Review analyst consensus — are estimates conservative or aggressive?
3. Look at recent guidance — did management raise or lower outlook?

**For stock price markets** (e.g., "Will [Stock] be above $X by [date]?"):
1. Check analyst price targets — is the target price above or below consensus?
2. Look at earnings date — a beat could push the stock to the target
3. Review insider activity — executives buying their own stock is meaningful

## Markets Where This Panel Activates

- Earnings beat/miss markets
- Stock price target markets
- M&A and acquisition markets
- CEO / leadership change markets
- Any market involving a publicly traded company
